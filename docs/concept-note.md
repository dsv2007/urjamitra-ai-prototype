# UrjaMitra AI Concept Note

## Project Title

UrjaMitra AI: AI-Powered Energy Twin and Machine Health Intelligence Platform for MSMEs

## Theme

Frontier Technology in MSMEs, with focus on energy efficiency, Industry 4.0/5.0 modernization, cost reduction and clean manufacturing.

Primary sub-theme: Energy Efficiency.

Secondary sub-theme: Industry 4.0 and 5.0.

## Problem Statement

Many MSMEs operate old or semi-automatic machines without real-time visibility into energy use, idle power wastage, overheating, vibration and maintenance risk. Owners often discover issues only after high electricity bills, sudden breakdown, production delay or expensive repair. Replacing machinery is costly, so MSMEs need an affordable modernization layer that works with existing machines.

## Proposed Solution

UrjaMitra AI installs a low-cost IoT sensor unit on existing machines and creates an AI Energy Twin for each machine. The platform monitors voltage, current, power, temperature, vibration, runtime, idle time, load and output. A rule-based AI engine detects overheating, high vibration, abnormal load, high power with low output and repeated warning patterns. The dashboard converts these readings into owner-friendly actions: maintenance urgency, expected savings, downtime avoided, carbon impact and ROI.

## Innovation

- AI Energy Twin for legacy MSME machines without machine replacement.
- Combined energy intelligence and predictive maintenance in one platform.
- Owner-friendly business language: saving possible, maintenance needed, power waste detected.
- Low-cost IoT architecture using ESP32, CT current sensor, voltage sensor, temperature sensor and vibration sensor.
- Rule-based AI prototype with a clear path to ML anomaly detection and federated benchmarking across sectors.
- Green impact reporting aligned with energy efficiency and sustainability goals.

## Technical Feasibility

The prototype uses simulated sensor data, a React dashboard, Express APIs, AI rules and database schemas. Hardware integration is feasible using ESP32 and MQTT/HTTP. The AI model can begin with rules and later train anomaly detection models using collected baseline data per machine type.

## Prototype Plan

1. Build live simulated IoT data stream for seven sample MSME machines.
2. Implement AI fault detection rules and maintenance priority engine.
3. Show AI Energy Twin dashboard with health, risk, wastage, carbon and savings.
4. Generate alert cards and recommendation cards.
5. Add ROI and green impact modules.
6. Provide backend APIs and database schema for future productization.
7. Demonstrate one high-risk machine triggering alert, recommendation, ROI and report.

- Marketing and branding: owner-friendly insights such as saving possible, maintenance needed and power waste detected.
- Technology: sensors, ESP32, APIs, AI rules, database and dashboard.

## Target MSME Users

Textile units, food processing units, fabrication shops, rice mills, cold storage, plastic moulding units, CNC workshops and packaging units.

## Expected Impact

- Reduction in electricity wastage through idle control and overload detection.
- Lower downtime through predictive maintenance alerts.
- Better productivity through machine-wise energy and output comparison.
- Improved sustainability reporting for MSME clusters.
- Affordable path to Industry 4.0 and 5.0 modernization.

## Commercialization Plan

- One-time IoT kit installation per machine cluster.
- Monthly dashboard subscription.
- Preventive maintenance support plan.
- Energy audit report service.
- Sector-specific customization for textile, rice mill, cold storage and fabrication clusters.

## Scalability

The system can scale from one MSME unit to cluster-level deployment. Multi-tenant cloud architecture can support machine benchmarking by sector while preserving unit-level privacy. Offline-first local gateway deployment can serve areas with unstable connectivity.

## Future Scope

- ML anomaly detection after collecting historical baseline data.
- Predictive remaining useful life estimation.
- WhatsApp/SMS alerts in local languages.
- Integration with smart relays for automatic idle cut-off.
- Cluster-level energy efficiency scoring.
- Carbon credit readiness and green finance reporting.
